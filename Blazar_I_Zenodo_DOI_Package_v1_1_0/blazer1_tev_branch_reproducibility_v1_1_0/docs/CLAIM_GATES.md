# Claim gates

- The active score is a population coordinate, not a detection probability.
- TeVCat recovery is retrospective and affected by historical target selection.
- Exact availability-pattern conditioning reduces, but does not eliminate, the observed separation.
- The report-date audit is quasi-prospective because historical feature snapshots were not reconstructed.
- The observed-control pilot has only two firm and two non-firm matched sources and is inconclusive.
- List A and List B are follow-up prioritization products, not detections.
- The FSRQ/QSO-like to HSP-like ordering is demographic and not direct temporal evolution.
- The 4LAC result is a non-overlapping external-cohort transfer audit, not fully independent validation.
- The 4LAC transfer supports ranking transportability; it does not establish universal X-ray dominance.
- The transferred coordinate does not have an established incremental advantage over the gamma-only baseline.
- Public eRASS1 coverage does not provide enough external firm TeV positives for a formal paired X-ray-only/active/no-X ordering test; no such external ordering claim is released.
